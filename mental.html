What is Mental Wellbeing?

Mental wellbeing is how we feel inside, its how we percieve happiness in our day to day lives. However, a large number of people suffer with poor mental health. It is important to state that this is not the fault of the person with poor mental health, as it can be caused by a wide range of things.

Fortunately, the conversation regarding mental health has become a lot more open in the past years, meaning more and more people are feeling comfortable about getting the help they need.

Depression

Depression is a constant feeling of sadness and loss of interest, which stops you doing your normal activities. This can come in many forms, such as a lack of energy or motivation to achieve daily tasks. Depression can also affect people's social lives as they may feel unable to participate in group activities.

Depression is often misread as the person being lazy or unmotivated, which means that it can lie hidden for years. This leads to further problems down the rode as stress and low mood can build up over the years.

Anxiety

Anxiety is an emotion characterized by feelings of tension, worried thoughts and physical changes like increased blood pressure. This can often make the person feel nauseas or skittish due to the adrenaline within their body. The stress can be caused by a wide number of things, ranging from a singular event within their life, or just anxious about general things.

There is medication available to help decrease the effect of anxiety on the body, which can be prescribed by your local GP. If you feel like you have symptoms of anxiety, phone your local GP and they will decide what is the best course of action.
